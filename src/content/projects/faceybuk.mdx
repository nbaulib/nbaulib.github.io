## Overview
A video chatting app that helps long-distance families stay connected through shared reading. 
Built during HackNYU Fall 2025.

## Problems
- Many families are separated by immigration, military deployment, or work schedules
- Declining literacy rates among young children
- Over-reliance on low-quality screen time

## Guiding Question 
**How might we make screen time more interactive to support child development and familial bonds?**

## Research
- The [American Academy of Pediatrics](https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/138/5/e20162591/60503/Media-and-Young-Minds) discourages screen time for children under 3, with the exception of video calls.
- [Co-viewing](https://www.healthychildren.org/English/family-life/Media/Pages/why-co-viewing-is-important-tips-to-share-screen-time-with-your-kids.aspx) with prompting increases information processing.
- 1 in 5 children enter kindergarten with reading skills [three years below their grade level](https://online.regiscollege.edu/blog/child-illiteracy).

**Conclusion:** Video calls + Reading = Ideal screentime 

## Solution
**On The Go Storyteller**  
- Parent and child join a synced reading session  
- Parent controls story pacing line-by-line  
- Text updates in real time across both screens  
- Characters animate and speak using text-to-speech  

This transforms everyday calls into guaranteed learning experiences.
